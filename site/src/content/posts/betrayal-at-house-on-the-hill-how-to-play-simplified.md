You're trapped inside a haunted house. Explore room by room, find items and omens — until someone triggers the Haunt, one player may turn traitor, and the game completely transforms.

> Our full review: [Betrayal at House on the Hill Review](/blog/betrayal-at-house-on-the-hill-review/)

> **Note:** These simplified rules help you get started quickly. Read the full rulebook before your first play — there are 50 different Haunts, each with unique objectives.

## The Big Picture

Betrayal has two phases:

**Phase 1 — Exploration:** Everyone cooperates, exploring the house tile by tile, collecting items, and surviving Event and Omen cards.

**Phase 2 — The Haunt:** When triggered, the game reveals which player (if any) is the Traitor. Traitor and Survivors read separate rulebooks with secret, usually opposing objectives.

## Setup

<div class="steps">
  <div class="step"><span class="step-num">1</span><div>Each player chooses a <strong>character card</strong> (each has two sides). Place four starting room tiles: the Upper Landing, Basement Landing, and Main Floor Entrance.</div></div>
  <div class="step"><span class="step-num">2</span><div>Shuffle the Event, Omen, and Item decks and place them face-down. Take out all the dice.</div></div>
  <div class="step"><span class="step-num">3</span><div>Set your character's stat markers (Speed, Might, Sanity, Knowledge) on the starting values shown on your card. Track these on paper or use the companion app (search "Betrayal Character Stats").</div></div>
  <div class="step"><span class="step-num">4</span><div>The player with the next upcoming birthday goes first.</div></div>
</div>

## Exploration Phase

<div class="steps">
  <div class="step"><span class="step-num">1</span><div><strong>Move</strong> up to as many rooms as your Speed stat allows. You can move through any open doorway.</div></div>
  <div class="step"><span class="step-num">2</span><div>When you enter an <strong>unexplored doorway</strong>, draw a room tile. Place it on the matching floor (rooms are labelled Upper, Middle, or Basement). If the tile doesn't fit the current floor, set it aside and keep drawing.</div></div>
  <div class="step"><span class="step-num">3</span><div>If the new room has a <strong>symbol</strong>, draw the matching card (Event, Item, or Omen) and follow its instructions.</div></div>
  <div class="step"><span class="step-num">4</span><div>After drawing an <strong>Omen card</strong>, make a Haunt Roll: roll 6 dice (faces 0–2). If your result is lower than the total number of Omen cards drawn so far, the Haunt begins.</div></div>
</div>

> **If your stat reaches the skull-and-crossbones, you die and are out of the game.**

## Starting the Haunt

When the Haunt is triggered:

<div class="steps">
  <div class="step"><span class="step-num">1</span><div>Open the <strong>Traitor's Tome</strong> and cross-reference the room where the Haunt started with the Omen that triggered it. This tells you who (if anyone) is the Traitor.</div></div>
  <div class="step"><span class="step-num">2</span><div>The Traitor leaves the room and reads their secret objective in the Traitor's Tome. Everyone else reads their objective together in the <strong>Secrets of Survival</strong> book.</div></div>
  <div class="step"><span class="step-num">3</span><div>Objectives are usually directly opposed — the Traitor wins if the Survivors fail, and vice versa.</div></div>
  <div class="step"><span class="step-num">4</span><div>Some Haunts introduce monsters. These are moved by the Traitor each round using the rules in their section of the Traitor's Tome.</div></div>
</div>

## Tips for New Players

- Track stats on paper — the card markers slip easily.
- The Haunt Rolls start low-risk but become likely after the fourth or fifth Omen card.
- Each of the 50 Haunts has completely different objectives. Read yours carefully before the battle phase begins.

<div class="faq">
  <details class="faq-item">
    <summary>Is there always a Traitor?</summary>
    <p>Not always. Some Haunts have no Traitor — the Survivors face a monster or scenario without anyone going evil.</p>
  </details>
  <details class="faq-item">
    <summary>Can dead players still participate?</summary>
    <p>No — if your stat reaches the skull, you're out. Some players use dead characters to observe and offer neutral commentary.</p>
  </details>
  <details class="faq-item">
    <summary>Can the Traitor lie to the group before the Haunt?</summary>
    <p>Yes — the Haunt assignment is secret until the roll, but there's nothing stopping you from being a suspicious character during exploration.</p>
  </details>
</div>
