> **Note:** This is a starting guide, not a complete rulebook. The Gallerist is complex — read the full rules before your first play. This helps you understand the flow before diving in.

## The Big Picture

The Gallerist is a worker placement game. On your turn, you move your **Gallerist pawn** to one of the four action centres and take one of two actions there. Everything — fame, influence, collectors — converts to money in the end. Highest money wins.

**The Kick-Out Mechanic:** When you move your pawn to an action centre, you may leave an assistant behind. If another player enters that space, your assistant is kicked out and can immediately take a special **Executive Action** (move visitors or assign to a contract). This keeps all players engaged between turns.

## Visitors (Meeples)

There are three types:

| Colour | Type | Role |
|--------|------|------|
| Pink | Visitors (VIPs) | General visitors |
| Brown | Investors | Provide specific bonuses |
| White | Collectors | Score points and increase artist fame |

Visitors start in the plaza bag. Use all visitors in a 4-player game; remove 2 for 3 players; remove 4 for 2 players or solo.

## The Four Action Centres

<div class="rule-cards">
  <div class="rule-card">
    <p class="rule-num">1. ARTIST COLONY</p>
    <p class="rule-title">Discover or promote artists</p>
    <p><strong>Discover:</strong> Take an artist tile from the board, pay influence, and start building their market. <strong>Promote:</strong> Pay influence to advance an artist's fame track — increasing the value of their artwork.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="rule-card">
    <p class="rule-num">2. GALLERY</p>
    <p class="rule-title">Buy art or hire assistants</p>
    <p><strong>Buy Art:</strong> Pay the purchase price and place artwork in your gallery. This increases the artist's fame. <strong>Hire Assistants:</strong> Bring a new assistant from the unemployment pool to your player board office.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="rule-card">
    <p class="rule-num">3. SALES OFFICE</p>
    <p class="rule-title">Sign contracts or sell art</p>
    <p><strong>Sign Contract:</strong> Take a contract card matching an art medium — this enables you to sell that type of art. <strong>Sell Art:</strong> Sell displayed artwork for money based on the artist's current fame value.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="rule-card">
    <p class="rule-num">4. INTERNATIONAL MARKET</p>
    <p class="rule-title">Gain reputation or bid on renowned works</p>
    <p><strong>Take Reputation Tile:</strong> Gain immediate and end-game bonuses. Requires matching sold/displayed art and lobby visitors. <strong>Bid on Art:</strong> Spend influence to acquire renowned artworks worth bonus VP.</p>
  </div>
</div>

## On Your Turn

<div class="steps">
  <div class="step"><span class="step-num">1</span><div>Move your <strong>Gallerist pawn</strong> to any action centre. You may leave an assistant behind.</div></div>
  <div class="step"><span class="step-num">2</span><div>Take the <strong>Bonus Tile</strong> if it's the first player to arrive at that space this round.</div></div>
  <div class="step"><span class="step-num">3</span><div>Choose <strong>one of the two actions</strong> at that location and resolve it.</div></div>
  <div class="step"><span class="step-num">4</span><div>Optionally take an <strong>Executive Action</strong> before or after your location action: move visitors (using matching tickets) or assign an assistant to a contract.</div></div>
</div>

## End of Game

The game ends when **two or more** of these are true:
- No more tickets remain
- No more visitors in the bag
- Two or more artists have reached Celebrity status

Count all money (from sales, end-game card bonuses, assistant bonuses in the International Market, and leftover resources). Highest total wins.

<div class="faq">
  <details class="faq-item">
    <summary>What is a Kicked-Out Action?</summary>
    <p>When another player moves to an action centre where your assistant sits, your assistant is kicked out. You immediately take one Executive Action (move visitors or assign an assistant to a contract) — even though it's not your turn.</p>
  </details>
  <details class="faq-item">
    <summary>Do I need to sell art to earn money?</summary>
    <p>Selling art is the main income source, but end-game bonuses from curator and art dealer cards, international market positions, and other bonuses also convert to money.</p>
  </details>
  <details class="faq-item">
    <summary>Can I move visitors from another player's lobby?</summary>
    <p>Yes — Executive Actions let you move visitors from any lobby (including opponents') to the plaza, another lobby, or your own gallery.</p>
  </details>
</div>
