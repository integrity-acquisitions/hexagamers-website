## Setup

<div class="steps">
  <div class="step"><span class="step-num">1</span><div>Deal each player a <strong>random Role card</strong>. There are 7 roles (Scientist, Medic, Researcher, etc.) with different special abilities.</div></div>
  <div class="step"><span class="step-num">2</span><div>Randomly select <strong>9 cities</strong> to be initially infected: 3 cities get 3 cubes, 3 get 2 cubes, 3 get 1 cube, each in the city's matching colour.</div></div>
  <div class="step"><span class="step-num">3</span><div>Choose your <strong>difficulty level</strong> by selecting 4, 5, or 6 Epidemic cards to shuffle into the player card deck using <strong>Pandemic Shuffling</strong> (see below).</div></div>
  <div class="step"><span class="step-num">4</span><div>All players start together in <strong>Atlanta</strong> at the first Research Station.</div></div>
</div>

## What is Pandemic Shuffling?

Instead of shuffling Epidemic cards directly into the full deck:

1. Split the player card deck into equal piles (one per Epidemic card).
2. Shuffle one Epidemic card into each pile.
3. Stack all piles together without re-shuffling.

This guarantees Epidemics are spread evenly across the game — but within each section they can still cluster. You might get two back-to-back, but never three in a row.

## How a Turn Works

Each player takes **4 actions** per turn, then draws 2 player cards, then draws Infection cards.

**Your 4 actions (spend any combination):**
- Move to an adjacent city
- Direct flight to any city (discard that city card)
- Charter a flight from a city you have the card for
- Build a Research Station (discard that city card)
- Treat disease (remove one cube from your current city)
- Share knowledge (trade a city card between players in the same city)
- Discover a cure (at a Research Station, discard 5 matching-colour city cards)

**After your 4 actions:**
- Draw 2 player cards. If you draw an **Epidemic card**: increase the infection rate, then shuffle all discarded infection cards and put them on TOP of the infection deck.
- Draw Infection cards equal to the current **infection rate**. Add one cube of matching colour to each drawn city.

## Outbreak and Chain Reactions

When a city would receive a 4th cube of the same colour, an **Outbreak** occurs:
- Move the Outbreak marker one space forward
- Every connected city receives one cube of that colour
- If any connected city also has 3 cubes, it chains into another Outbreak

> **8 outbreaks = you lose.**

## How to Win

Cure all 4 diseases. To cure a disease: discard **5 city cards of the same colour** (4 if you're the Scientist) while at a Research Station.

Once cured, that disease can be eradicated by removing all remaining cubes. An eradicated disease never spreads again.

## How to Lose

You lose immediately if any of these happen:
- 8 outbreaks occur
- Any colour runs out of disease cubes
- The player card deck is empty and someone must draw

<div class="faq">
  <details class="faq-item">
    <summary>What's the difference between treating and curing?</summary>
    <p>Treating removes one cube from a city per action (the Medic removes all cubes for one action). Curing means the disease is officially discovered — once cured, any treated cube of that colour is immediately eradicated from the board.</p>
  </details>
  <details class="faq-item">
    <summary>Can players share their cards freely?</summary>
    <p>No — you can only share cards if both players are in the same city AND the card matches that city. The Researcher is an exception and can share any city card.</p>
  </details>
  <details class="faq-item">
    <summary>Do we have to eradicate all diseases to win?</summary>
    <p>No. You only need to cure all 4 diseases. Eradication is a bonus that prevents a cured disease from spreading further — powerful but not required to win.</p>
  </details>
</div>
