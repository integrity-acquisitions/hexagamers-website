Connect cities across Europe with coloured trains to complete routes and score points. Longer routes are worth more points. But watch out — rivals may be racing for the same cities, forcing you to find alternate paths.

> Full thoughts here: [Ticket to Ride: Europe – Review](/blog/ticket-to-ride-europe-review/)

> Also on our list: [Best Board Games for Beginners](/blog/best-gateway-board-games-for-beginners-with-reviews/)

> **Note:** This guide covers the core rules to get you started — not every edge case. Read the rulebook for full detail.

## The Objective

Connect two cities on a Destination Route card using a chain of coloured train segments on the board. Completing routes earns points. Failing to complete a route by the end of the game costs you points equal to its value.

## Setup

<div class="steps">
  <div class="step"><span class="step-num">1</span><div>Lay out the game board. Each player takes their trains, train stations, and score marker.</div></div>
  <div class="step"><span class="step-num">2</span><div>Deal each player <strong>4 Destination Route cards</strong> (1 long, 3 short). Players must keep at least 2 — return any they don't want, face-down.</div></div>
  <div class="step"><span class="step-num">3</span><div>Deal each player <strong>3 Train Car cards</strong> to start their hand.</div></div>
  <div class="step"><span class="step-num">4</span><div>Place <strong>5 Train Car cards face-up</strong> in the centre. The remainder form a face-down draw pile beside them.</div></div>
</div>

## On Your Turn — Choose 1 Action

<div class="rule-cards">
  <div class="rule-card">
    <p class="rule-num">Action 1</p>
    <p class="rule-title">Draw Train Car Cards</p>
    <p>Take 2 cards from the face-up display or draw pile (your choice). If you take a face-up Locomotive (wild card), that counts as both draws — you get only 1 card total.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="rule-card">
    <p class="rule-num">Action 2</p>
    <p class="rule-title">Claim a Route</p>
    <p>Play matching Train Car cards equal to the number of spaces between two cities. The route is now yours — no one else can use it.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="rule-card">
    <p class="rule-num">Action 3</p>
    <p class="rule-title">Draw Route Cards</p>
    <p>Draw 3 Destination Route cards and keep at least 1. Uncompleted routes count as negative points at game end.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="rule-card">
    <p class="rule-num">Action 4</p>
    <p class="rule-title">Place a Train Station</p>
    <p>Costs Train Car cards (1 card for your 1st station, 2 for your 2nd, 3 for your 3rd). A station lets you use one of another player's routes to complete your own destination.</p>
  </div>
</div>

## Special Route Types

| Route Type | Rule |
|---|---|
| **Grey** | Use any single colour — all cards played must match each other |
| **Ferry** | Requires at least 1 (sometimes more) Locomotive card to complete |
| **Underground (Tunnel)** | After playing your cards, flip 3 from the draw pile — if any match your colour (including Locomotives), you must play extra cards or abandon the route |

## Scoring

**During the game** — when you claim a route, score points immediately based on length:

| Route Length | Points |
|---|---|
| 1 | 1 |
| 2 | 2 |
| 3 | 4 |
| 4 | 7 |
| 6 | 15 |
| 8 | 21 |

**End of game bonuses:**
- Completed Destination Route cards: **+card value**
- Uncompleted Destination Route cards: **−card value**
- Longest continuous train: **+10 points** (train stations don't count toward this)
- Each unused train station remaining: **+4 points**

## End of Game

When any player has **2 or fewer trains left**, finish the current round so everyone gets one more turn. Then total all points.
