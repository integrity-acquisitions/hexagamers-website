Two spy teams compete to contact all their agents first. Your Spymaster gives one-word clues linking multiple codenames on the grid — but guess wrong and you might hand the game to your rivals, or hit the assassin and lose instantly.

<div class="game-stats">
  <div class="stat"><span class="stat-value">2 – 8</span><span class="stat-label">Players</span></div>
  <div class="stat"><span class="stat-value">15–30 min</span><span class="stat-label">Play Time</span></div>
  <div class="stat"><span class="stat-value">14+</span><span class="stat-label">Age</span></div>
</div>

## Setup

<div class="steps">
  <div class="step"><span class="step-num">1</span><div>Lay out <strong>25 word cards</strong> in a 5×5 grid in the centre of the table.</div></div>
  <div class="step"><span class="step-num">2</span><div>Divide into <strong>two teams</strong>. Each team picks a Spymaster. Spymasters sit on the same side of the table, opposite their teams.</div></div>
  <div class="step"><span class="step-num">3</span><div>Draw a random <strong>key card</strong> and place it in the Spymaster stand so only Spymasters can see it. This card shows which words belong to which team and where the assassin is.</div></div>
  <div class="step"><span class="step-num">4</span><div>The team whose colour appears in the <strong>corner of the key card</strong> goes first and must contact one extra agent.</div></div>
</div>

## How a Turn Works

<div class="steps">
  <div class="step"><span class="step-num">1</span><div>The active Spymaster gives a <strong>one-word clue</strong> followed by a number: the clue word plus how many cards on the grid it relates to. Example: <em>"Animals: 3"</em></div></div>
  <div class="step"><span class="step-num">2</span><div>The team discusses and <strong>touches one card</strong> they believe matches the clue.</div></div>
  <div class="step"><span class="step-num">3</span><div>The Spymaster places the appropriate token on that card: agent (your colour), rival agent, innocent bystander, or <strong>assassin</strong>.</div></div>
  <div class="step"><span class="step-num">4</span><div>If correct, the team may guess again. They must guess at least once, can guess up to <strong>the given number + 1</strong>, and may stop at any time.</div></div>
  <div class="step"><span class="step-num">5</span><div>If the team guesses a bystander, rival agent, or stops guessing — the turn passes to the other team.</div></div>
</div>

## Win and Lose Conditions

<div class="rule-cards">
  <div class="rule-card">
    <p class="rule-num">WIN</p>
    <p class="rule-title">Contact all your agents</p>
    <p>The first team to uncover all their agents on the grid wins the round.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="rule-card">
    <p class="rule-num">INSTANT LOSS</p>
    <p class="rule-title">Hit the assassin</p>
    <p>If any team touches the assassin card, that team immediately loses — no matter how many agents remain.</p>
  </div>
</div>

## Spymaster Rules

- Give only **one word** and a number. No gestures, reactions, or bonus hints.
- The clue cannot be a word on the board (or a derivative of one).
- You may not respond to your team's guesses — stay stone-faced.

<div class="faq">
  <details class="faq-item">
    <summary>Can the Spymaster use proper nouns?</summary>
    <p>Yes — place names, brand names, and people's names are all valid clues as long as they're not a word currently on the grid.</p>
  </details>
  <details class="faq-item">
    <summary>Can we use the extra guess on a different clue from a previous turn?</summary>
    <p>Yes — the +1 extra guess can be used on any unresolved word from a previous clue, not just the current one.</p>
  </details>
  <details class="faq-item">
    <summary>What if the Spymaster accidentally reacts to a guess?</summary>
    <p>House rule it — most groups give a warning on the first accidental reaction. The Spymaster role is legitimately hard.</p>
  </details>
</div>
